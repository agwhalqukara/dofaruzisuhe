FAQs about Michael Crawford

Who is Michael Crawford?

Michael Crawford, born Michael Patrick Smith on January 19, 1942, is an English actor, comedian, and singer renowned for his versatility across TV, film, and stage performances.

He gained fame for his role as Frank Spencer in the British television sitcom Some Mothers Do ‘Ave ‘Em and as the original Phantom in Andrew Lloyd Webber’s The Phantom of the Opera.

What role made him famous?

He became a household name playing the bumbling and accident-prone Frank Spencer in the popular British TV sitcom Some Mothers Do ‘Ave ‘Em, which first aired in the 1970s.

Has he won any awards for his acting?

Yes, he has received several prestigious awards throughout his career, including a Tony Award and a Laurence Olivier Award for his iconic role in The Phantom of the Opera.

Did he perform his own stunts in Some Mothers Do ‘Ave ‘Em?

He famously performed his own stunts in Some Mothers Do ‘Ave ‘Em, which added to the physical comedy of his character Frank Spencer’s numerous mishaps.
