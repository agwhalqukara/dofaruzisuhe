Hozier’s Career

  • Hozier’s journey as an Irish musician and singer had its roots in his college years.
  • He began his formal music education at St. Gerard’s School and continued his studies at Trinity College University in Dublin.
  • During his time at Trinity, he actively participated in the Trinity Orchestra and had the privilege of performing alongside the renowned vocal ensemble Anúna.
  • Hozier’s breakthrough moment arrived in 2013 with the release of his iconic and critically acclaimed song, “Take Me to Church.”
  • The song quickly became a sensation, thanks in part to Stephen Fry’s endorsement, and it rapidly ascended the global music charts.
  • Notably, “Take Me to Church” achieved the remarkable feat of reaching the second position on the Billboard Hot 100 chart, further solidifying Hozier’s status as a musical force to be reckoned with.
  • The accompanying music video for “Take Me to Church” added another layer of significance to the song’s impact.
  • The video, which directly addressed issues of discrimination within the Catholic Church, particularly relating to the LGBTQ+ community, went viral on platforms such as YouTube and Reddit, amplifying the song’s message and reach.
  • Hozier’s debut extended play, also bearing the title “Take Me to Church,” was released on July 3, 2013, and made its presence felt on the Billboard 200 chart, further enhancing his musical legacy.
